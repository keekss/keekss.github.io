Two comparisons jump to mind when thinking of design patterns: music theory and storytelling templates.  Both are forms followed in quality work, whether consciously or not.  Pioneers and/or veterans of their respective fields identified these commonalities, described them, discussed them, and shared them, so that others of lesser experience could make the most of their efforts.

In the early stages of music, people just made sounds they liked.  And of course, such is still often the end goal, but the guidance of music theory, and more casually, musical genres, guide the modern songwriting process.  Music is such an emotional endeavour that people often obey the laws of musical theories and genres without realizing it.  A reggae artist may instinctively put emphasis on the upbeats of songs, knowing simply that it sounds right.
